Level 1

Easy

Easy is the best way to learn how 3D piece movement works. The AI intentionally makes imprecise moves, giving you room to experiment with vertical attacks, multi-layer pins, and 3D forks. Level 2

Medium

Medium provides solid opposition. The engine searches deeper and punishes obvious blunders, but still leaves openings you can exploit if you read the board well. This is where you start developing real 3D intuition—learning which layers to control, when to advance vertically, and how to coordinate attacks across multiple planes.

Level 3

Hard

Hard plays aggressively with deep analysis, searching further ahead and finding tactical sequences across all three dimensions. Expect the AI to exploit unguarded layers, launch multi-directional knight forks, and control vertical space ruthlessly. Even experienced players will find this a worthy free chess opponent.
